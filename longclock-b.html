The Long Clock

On monetary power in a multipolar age

Reserve money is a regime, and regimes end.

Reserve currency feels permanent to the people living under it, and it never is. It is a regime – held up by trade, by navies, by trust – and every regime so far has given way to the next. Each lasted a few generations. Each felt eternal. Each ended.

1600sThe Dutch guilder – the first true global trading currency
1800sPound sterling – empire, gold standard, the City of London
1944The US dollar – Bretton Woods; the dollar pegged to gold
1971Dollar unmoored – Nixon ends gold convertibility; pure fiat begins
202_A transition you can already feel

You already believe we are living inside one of those transitions. The part worth your attention is what the issuing power reaches for once it believes it too. Keep reading – the ground is about to shift under your feet.
